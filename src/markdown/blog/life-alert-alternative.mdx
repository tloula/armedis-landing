## Why People Search for Life Alert Alternatives

Life Alert is one of the most recognized names in senior safety, and for good reason — it's been around since 1987 and has helped real people in real emergencies. But many people who search for "Life Alert alternative" are realizing that a medical alert device isn't quite what they're looking for:

- **It's expensive** — Life Alert starts at ~$69.95/month for the basic in-home plan, rising to ~$89.95/month for the GPS bundle, plus a one-time activation fee around $197 just to get started. That's nearly **$200 before you've used the service for a single day** — compared to CheckIn More's 14-day free trial with no upfront cost whatsoever.
- **It requires hardware** — you need a base station at home and a wearable button (or mobile GPS device)
- **It requires a 3-year contract** — and Life Alert is one of the last major providers that still locks customers into a multi-year commitment. Most competitors have moved to month-to-month plans.
- **It doesn't publish prices online** — you have to call their sales team to find out what you'll pay
- **It doesn't offer fall detection** — despite its premium price, Life Alert is one of the few providers that offers no fall detection on any plan
- **It doesn't create a daily routine** — Life Alert responds when you press a button; it doesn't give your family a regular signal that everything's okay
- **It uses a call center, not your family** — when you press the button, you reach a dispatcher, not your daughter or your neighbor
- **It can feel clinical** — wearing a medical alert device stigmatizes independence in a way a check-in app doesn't

For many people living alone — including a lot of seniors who are healthy and active — a daily wellness check-in is the better fit.

## Who Might Prefer Life Alert?

Life Alert exists for a specific, important reason: if someone **cannot reach their phone** in an emergency — whether from a fall, a medical episode, or mobility limitations — a wearable button is the right tool.

If the person you're worried about:
- Has limited mobility or a history of falls
- May not be able to reach their phone during an emergency
- Is at high medical risk and needs professional dispatch, not just a family alert
- Doesn't use a smartphone

...then Life Alert (or a similar PERS device) may be the more appropriate choice. Note that if automatic fall detection is important to you, Life Alert does **not** offer this feature on any plan — you'd want to consider alternatives like Medical Guardian or Bay Alarm Medical that do.

The two tools can also be used together — Life Alert for emergency response, CheckIn More for daily family connection.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does CheckIn More require any special hardware?**
No. CheckIn More runs on any iOS or Android smartphone you already own. No base station, no wearable button, no installation.

**How does CheckIn More notify contacts when I miss a check-in?**
Depending on your plan: email, SMS text, or phone call. Your contacts don't need to download anything.

**What plans does CheckIn More offer?**
CheckIn More offers three plans: Email, SMS (available now with a 14-day free trial), and Voice. Plans are cumulative — SMS includes email, and Voice includes email and SMS.

**Can I pause my check-ins?**
Yes. You can pause check-ins for vacations or any schedule change without canceling your subscription.

**Is CheckIn More a replacement for a medical alert device?**
Not for high-risk situations. If your loved one could fall and be unable to reach their phone, a wearable medical alert device is the right tool. CheckIn More is for independent people who want a daily safety net that keeps family informed — it's a complement to, not a replacement for, emergency hardware in high-risk cases.

**Does CheckIn More work if I can't reach my phone?**
No — CheckIn More requires you to interact with your smartphone to check in. A wearable device like Life Alert is better suited for situations where you might not be able to reach your phone.
