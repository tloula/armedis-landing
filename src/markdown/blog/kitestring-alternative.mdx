## What Kitestring Did

Kitestring was straightforward. You'd set a timer before heading out alone — a run, a hike, a first date, a walk home at night. If you didn't check back in when the timer expired, Kitestring would text your emergency contacts.

That's it. No GPS. No location sharing. Just a countdown and an SMS safety net.

It was a genuinely good idea, built as a website and SMS service at a time when personal safety apps were rare. Thousands of people used it. And then it disappeared.

When your timer expires, CheckIn More waits **5 minutes** before notifying your contacts. Life doesn't run on the minute — the timer ending doesn't mean something is wrong, it means you haven't checked in yet. The grace period keeps things from feeling like a false alarm every time you're slightly late.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why did Kitestring shut down?**
Kitestring launched in 2014 as a web and SMS-based safety service. It was never formally acquired or merged — it simply stopped operating around February 2024. No official shutdown announcement was made. The domain is no longer active as a safety service.

**Does CheckIn More work the same way as Kitestring?**
The core mechanic is identical: set a timer, check back in when you're safe, and your contacts are notified if you don't. CheckIn More adds a native mobile app, email notifications, optional phone call alerts, and a full daily check-in system.

**Do my contacts need to download anything?**
No. Your contacts receive alerts by email, SMS, or phone call. No app, no account required on their end.

**Can I manage timers over text message?**
Yes, on Plus and Premium plans. You can start, extend, and cancel timers entirely via SMS — no need to open the app.

**Is there a free plan?**
Yes. The free plan includes one safety timer per day and email alerts to one contact. No credit card required.
