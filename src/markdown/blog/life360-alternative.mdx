## Why Adults Living Alone Search for Life360 Alternatives

Life360 is genuinely useful software — but it's designed around a specific model: a family circle where everyone has the app installed, location is always shared, and the focus is on teens and driving.

That creates friction for adults who just want a low-key daily check-in:

- **Everyone needs the app** — your contacts must download Life360 and join your Circle to get alerts
- **Always-on GPS** — location is shared continuously, not just during a scheduled check-in window
- **No check-in ritual** — Life360 tracks passively; there's no deliberate "I'm okay" moment
- **Not built for solo living** — crash detection and speed alerts are great for drivers, not for someone who wants a daily wellness signal
- **Price** — Life360 Gold runs $14.99/month for features you mostly don't need in this context

CheckIn More takes a different approach entirely.

## How CheckIn More Is Different

### <Icon name="clock" /> It's Built Around a Daily Ritual, Not Passive Surveillance

CheckIn More works like an alarm you confirm. You set a check-in time (or several), and when the window comes around, you tap to say you're okay — or share how you're actually feeling. If you don't check in, your contacts are notified.

That's it. No GPS. No background tracking. No one watching where you went for lunch.

### <Icon name="map-pin-off" /> No Location Permissions Required

Life360's entire product is built on "always on" location access. CheckIn More doesn't request location permissions at all. The app runs on your schedule, not your coordinates.

For many users — especially those with privacy concerns or who simply don't want to feel monitored — this is the difference-maker.

### <Icon name="users" /> Your Contacts Don't Need to Download Anything

With Life360, everyone in your circle needs the app. With CheckIn More, your emergency contacts just need a phone. They receive alerts via email, SMS, or phone call depending on your preferences — no account, no app, no setup required on their end.

That's a much lower barrier for an elderly parent's adult children, or a solo traveler's family back home.
