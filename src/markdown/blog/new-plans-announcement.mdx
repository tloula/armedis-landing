We're excited to announce two new additions to CheckIn More: a **free plan** and a **Premium plan** — along with a new anomalous missed check-in detection feature that comes with all plans.

Here's what's new and what it means for you.

---

## <Icon name="gift" /> A Free Plan, Because Peace of Mind Shouldn't Have a Paywall

CheckIn More now has a genuinely free tier. No trial period, no credit card required.

The free plan includes:
- **1 daily check-in time**
- **Email notifications** to 1 contact
- Mood status check-ins and assistance request button

We're able to offer this for free because email is a fundamentally cheaper notification channel than SMS or phone calls — sending an email costs a fraction of a cent, while texts and calls carry real per-message costs. That difference lets us offer a useful, fully functional plan at no cost, and only charge when we're incurring costs to deliver something more.

If one daily check-in and a single contact is all you need, the free plan may be all you ever need.

---

## <Icon name="phone-call" /> Premium Plan: Phone Calls for the Moments That Matter

The new **Premium plan** ($19.99/month) adds AI phone calls to your contacts when you miss a check-in.

Why does that matter? A missed check-in email is easy to overlook. A text is better. But a phone call is harder to miss — and for the people who care about you most, that urgency is sometimes exactly what the situation calls for.

Premium plan includes everything in Plus (email + SMS notifications, unlimited check-ins, unlimited contacts), and adds:
- **AI phone call to contacts** on a missed check-in

---

## <Icon name="shield-alert" /> New Feature: Anomalous Missed Check-in Detection

All three plans now include a new smart alerting feature: **anomalous missed check-in detection**.

Here's how it works: CheckIn More learns your check-in patterns over time. If it detects an unusual number of missed check-ins — beyond what's normal for you — it can trigger an additional, escalated notification to your contacts.

This pairs naturally with the notification tiers. For example:

- **Missed check-in** → SMS text to contacts
- **Anomalous pattern detected** → Phone call to contacts

And on the free plan, this means you can have a setup where email is only sent when something genuinely unusual is happening — a quiet, low-friction safety net that only speaks up when it's worth paying attention to.

For most people, a missed check-in is a forgotten phone, a late errand, or a nap that ran long. An anomalous pattern is something worth paying closer attention to. This feature helps the people who care about you tell the difference — without flooding them with alerts for every minor slip.
