## The Key Differences

### <Icon name="shield" /> Reactive vs. Proactive Safety

This is the core distinction.

**bSafe** is a reactive safety app. Its headline features — SOS activation, live video streaming, voice-triggered alerts, GPS location sharing — are designed for emergencies in progress. The app is built around the assumption that something might be happening right now, and you need to alert people fast and create evidence.

**CheckIn More** is a proactive safety app. It's built around scheduled routines and safety timers: you check in regularly so your people know you're okay, and you set a timer when heading somewhere alone so someone's watching out for you. Nothing has to go wrong for it to be useful.

Neither approach is wrong — they're solving different problems.

### <Icon name="map-pin" /> Location Tracking

bSafe requires "always on" location permissions. Your GPS position is shared with Guardians in real time, and dispatched to them automatically if you trigger SOS.

CheckIn More doesn't request location permissions at all. Your contacts learn whether you checked in or not — not where you are. This is a meaningful privacy distinction for users who want a safety net without constant location surveillance.

### <Icon name="circle-dollar-sign" /> What's Actually Free

Both apps advertise a free tier. The difference is what you get.

bSafe's free plan locks most of the meaningful features — including reliable SOS functionality — behind a premium subscription. User reviews consistently report discovering this at the worst possible moment.

CheckIn More's free plan includes one daily check-in, one safety timer per day, and email alerts to one contact. You're prompted to subscribe during app setup, not during an emergency.

### <Icon name="calendar" /> Daily Check-In Schedules

bSafe doesn't have a recurring check-in system. It's designed for active situations, not daily wellness routines.

CheckIn More lets you build a full check-in schedule — different times per day of week, multiple check-ins daily, a customizable window before contacts are notified, and the ability to pause for travel. It's an ongoing safety net, not just an emergency tool.

### <Icon name="message-square" /> SMS Timer Management

On Plus and Premium plans, CheckIn More lets you manage safety timers entirely over text — no app needed:

- **"45m going on a run"**
- **"date night, back by 10:30pm"**
- **"running late, need 30 more minutes"**
- **"just got home"** (cancels the timer)

bSafe doesn't offer this.

### <Icon name="video" /> Video Streaming and SOS

bSafe's strongest differentiators are features CheckIn More doesn't have: voice-activated SOS, live video streaming to contacts, and automatic audio/video recording when an SOS is triggered. These are genuinely powerful for high-threat situations where you need to create evidence or summon help without opening an app.

If those capabilities are your primary need, bSafe's concept is worth considering — though user reviews raise consistent concerns about reliability and cost.

---

## Which One Is Right for You?

**Choose CheckIn More if** you want a daily check-in routine, an easy safety timer for solo activities, and a privacy-first approach with no location tracking. The free plan is genuinely useful, and paid plans add SMS alerts and phone calls to unlimited contacts.

**Choose bSafe if** your priority is a reactive panic button with live video evidence and voice-activated SOS. Understand going in that core features require a paid subscription, and that GPS reliability issues are a recurring complaint.

For most people who want to feel more connected and looked after — not just prepared for worst-case scenarios — CheckIn More is the more practical, everyday choice.
