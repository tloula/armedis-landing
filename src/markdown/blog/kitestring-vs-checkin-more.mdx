## What Was Kitestring?

Kitestring was a web-based personal safety service. The concept was simple:

- Tell Kitestring you're going somewhere alone and for how long
- When the timer expired, Kitestring would text you to check in
- If you didn't respond, it texted your emergency contacts

No GPS. No app to download. Just a website and SMS. It was used by runners, hikers, solo travelers, women going on first dates, and anyone who wanted a quiet safety net for the moments that called for one.

It worked well for what it was — a lightweight, no-fuss timer that kept someone informed if you didn't come home.

## The Key Differences

### SMS vs App

Kitestring was web and SMS-only — you managed everything through a browser or text message. CheckIn More is a native mobile app - you can manage timers from the app or over SMS. For most people, having an app is more convenient; for others, the Kitestring-style "just text it" workflow was a feature in itself. CheckIn More supports both.

### Notification Channels

Kitestring sent SMS alerts to your contacts when you missed a check-in. CheckIn More gives you three layers: email, SMS, and AI phone call. Email is less intrusive for routine check-ins; a phone call is appropriate for the moments where you really need someone to notice.

### Free Plan

Kitestring's free plan allowed 3 timers per month. CheckIn More's free plan includes 1 timer per day — more generous for regular use, and enough to cover most people's everyday situations.

### Daily Check-ins

Kitestring was timer-only — it had no concept of a scheduled daily check-in. CheckIn More combines Safety Timers with a full daily wellness check-in system. If you want both routine daily oversight and on-demand timers for specific activities, it's all in one place.
